## Platform highlights

<CardGroup cols={3}>
  <Card title="FHIR R4 Native" icon="layer-group">
    Built on HL7 FHIR R4. Use familiar resources — Patient, Observation,
    CarePlan — without learning a proprietary data model.
  </Card>
  <Card title="HIPAA-Ready" icon="shield-check">
    SOC 2 Type II certified. BAAs available for all plans. PHI stays
    encrypted at rest (AES-256) and in transit (TLS 1.2+).
  </Card>
  <Card title="Real-Time Events" icon="bolt">
    Subscribe to webhooks for patient updates, appointment changes,
    and EHR sync completions — no polling needed.
  </Card>
  <Card title="9 EHR Connectors" icon="plug">
    Pre-built adapters for Epic, Cerner, Athenahealth, eClinicalWorks,
    Meditech, and any SMART on FHIR system.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Multi-Tenant" icon="building">
    Manage multiple health system connections from one organization.
    Strict data isolation between tenants.
  </Card>
  <Card title="SDKs" icon="code">
    Official Python and Node.js SDKs. Auto-generated types from
    our OpenAPI spec for TypeScript projects.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Who uses Bramble Health

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Health Systems">
    Connect your existing EHR to a modern API layer. Surface structured
    clinical data to care management applications, patient portals, and
    analytics pipelines without ripping out legacy systems.

    **Common use cases:**
    - Unified patient search across multiple EHR instances
    - Real-time appointment sync to scheduling apps
    - Automated care-gap detection via observation feeds
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Digital Health Companies">
    Skip the FHIR integration complexity. Plug Bramble's API into your
    product and get normalized patient data from day one — across any
    EHR your customers use.

    **Common use cases:**
    - Remote patient monitoring apps
    - Medication adherence platforms
    - Chronic disease management tools
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Care Management Orgs">
    Coordinate care across a patient's full care team — PCPs, specialists,
    home health, and social services — through shared care plans and
    real-time observation feeds.

    **Common use cases:**
    - Complex care management programs
    - Transitional care coordination
    - Value-based care reporting
  </Tab>
</Tabs>
